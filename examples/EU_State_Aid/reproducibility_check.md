# Reproducibility

## Objective

To verify whether the mechanism identified by the Structural Triangulation Method (STM) remains stable across independent runs.

---

## Experimental Setup

The STM analysis was conducted across multiple independent runs with varying conditions:

* different languages
* different framework configurations (full and simplified)
* different model compositions and aggregation strategies

Each run analyzed the same regulatory system under different analytical conditions.

---

## Result

Across all runs, the same core mechanism was consistently identified:

```text id="r0m8tf"
complexity → discretion → information asymmetry → selectivity → concentration of benefits
```

---

## Structural Characteristics

Across multiple runs, the mechanism consistently includes additional structural detail:

* a key structural node:
  **the “notify / not notify” decision point (GBER / self-assessment)**

* localization of the mechanism at the **pre-notification stage**

* formation of a **weak-control zone prior to Commission scrutiny**

These elements are part of the system structure and do not contradict the core mechanism.

---

## Interpretation of Variation

Across runs:

* no structural contradictions are observed
* variations occur only at the level of detail
* additional elements refine the mechanism without altering its core

The result is based on **structural convergence across runs**, not on numerical averaging.

---

## Robustness

The mechanism remains stable despite variations in:

* analytical framework completeness
* language of execution
* model selection and weighting

Quantitative differences do not affect the structural outcome.
