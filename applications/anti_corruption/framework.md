
# Structural Triangulation Method (STM)

## Overview

The Structural Triangulation Method (STM) is an analytical framework designed to identify structural mechanisms in complex systems.

The method focuses on:

* structural factors
* interaction patterns
* reproducible mechanisms

STM is domain-independent and can be applied to different types of systems.

---

## Core Principles

STM is based on:

* multi-model triangulation
* independence of analysis
* structural convergence

The goal is to identify stable system behavior rather than individual interpretations.

---

## Analytical Dimensions

STM evaluates systems across a set of structural factors:

* F1 — Discretion
* F2 — Incentives
* F3 — Concentration of Benefits
* F4 — Access Points / Bottlenecks
* F5 — Enforcement Gap
* F6 — Transparency
* F7 — Information Control
* F8 — Complexity
* F9 — Selectivity
* F10 — Structural Risk
* F11 — Sanctions

These factors are abstract and can be applied across domains.

---

## Mechanism Extraction

STM identifies:

* causal chains
* interaction loops
* structural nodes

The focus is on mechanisms that:

* repeat across analyses
* remain stable under variation
* explain system-level outcomes

---

## Reproducibility

STM emphasizes:

* independent analytical runs
* consistency of results
* convergence of structure

Structural reproducibility is a core validation criterion.

---

# Application: Anti-Corruption Analysis

## Scope

In this repository, STM is applied to the analysis of **regulatory systems and potential corruption risks**.

---

## Interpretation of Factors

Within the anti-corruption domain:

* Discretion (F1) reflects decision-making flexibility
* Incentives (F2) reflect pressure or motivation for manipulation
* Information Control (F7) reflects asymmetry and control over evidence
* Enforcement Gap (F5) reflects gaps between formal rules and real enforcement

Other factors are interpreted accordingly in the context of systemic risk.

---

## Objective

The goal of the analysis is to identify:

* structural conditions enabling corruption-like behavior
* system-level vulnerabilities
* divergence between formal compliance and actual outcomes

---

## Output in This Context

In anti-corruption applications, STM produces:

* structural risk mechanisms
* identification of critical control points
* analysis of information asymmetry
* detection of potential compliance distortions

---

## Note

STM itself is domain-independent.

This repository demonstrates its application to anti-corruption analysis of regulatory systems.
