# Multi-Case Validation

## Objective

To validate whether the mechanism identified by STM is observable in real-world cases.

---

## STM Mechanism

```text
complexity → interpretability → self-reporting → information control → limited verification → compliance optimization
```

---

## Structural Refinement

Further STM analysis identified a critical structural node:

```text
self-assessment of "appropriateness"
```

This node determines how companies define the scope and intensity of due diligence measures within their supply chains.

---

## Case 1: Risk Analysis and Reporting

* Complex supply chain structures → ✔
* Internal interpretation of risk ("appropriateness") → ✔
* Company-generated reporting → ✔
* Limited external verification → ✔
* Variation in reported risk exposure → ✔

---

## Case 2: Supplier Monitoring

* Multi-tier supply chain complexity → ✔
* Conditional obligations for indirect suppliers → ✔
* Reliance on internal data and monitoring systems → ✔
* Selective depth of due diligence → ✔
* Limited external visibility into deeper tiers → ✔

---

## Case 3: Complaint Mechanisms

* Internal grievance mechanisms → ✔
* Information processed within company structures → ✔
* Limited external escalation → ✔
* Variation in responsiveness and outcomes → ✔

---

## Cross-Case Observation

Across all cases:

* the same structural pattern is observed
* companies control primary information flows
* interpretation of compliance is internally determined
* external verification is limited relative to system complexity
* outcomes vary depending on internal assessment

Additionally:

* depth of verification varies significantly
* not all risks are equally observable externally

---

## Structural Observation

The system creates a condition where:

* compliance is assessed through internally generated data
* external validation is partial
* reported compliance may not fully reflect underlying conditions

This corresponds to a **limited-verification zone within the compliance process**, consistent with the STM mechanism.

---

## Conclusion

The mechanism identified by STM is:

* reproducible across model-based analysis
* observable in real-world practices
* consistent across different components of the system

The analysis indicates that:

* formal regulatory requirements are present
* however, system behavior is shaped by internal information control

STM captures this as a structural configuration enabling **compliance optimization under constrained verification conditions**.
