# Reproducibility

## Objective

To verify whether the mechanism identified by the Structural Triangulation Method (STM) remains stable across independent runs.

---

## Experimental Setup

The STM analysis was conducted across multiple independent runs under controlled independence conditions:

* each run was performed in a separate chat session
* no memory of previous runs was used
* prompts explicitly instructed models not to rely on prior context
* runs were conducted at different times
* no iterative refinement between runs was allowed

Each run analyzed the same regulatory system under varying analytical conditions.

---

## Result

Across all runs, the same core mechanism was consistently identified:

```text
complexity → interpretability → self-reporting → information control → limited verification → compliance optimization
```

---

## Structural Characteristics

Across multiple runs, the mechanism consistently includes additional structural detail:

* a key structural node:
  **self-assessment of "appropriateness"**

* localization of the mechanism at the **internal compliance stage**

* formation of a **limited-verification zone between reporting and enforcement**

These elements are part of the system structure and do not contradict the core mechanism.

---

## Interpretation of Variation

Across runs:

* no structural contradictions are observed
* variations occur only at the level of factor intensity
* additional elements refine the mechanism without altering its core

The result is based on **structural convergence across runs**, not on numerical averaging.

Additionally:

The observed variation in certain factors reflects system-level complexity rather than analytical inconsistency.

While the structural mechanism remains stable across runs, parameter values depend on interpretation of enforcement intensity and information asymmetry.

This indicates a system with **stable structure but flexible operational realization**.

---

## Robustness

The mechanism remains stable despite variations in:

* analytical configuration
* language of execution
* model selection and aggregation

Quantitative differences do not affect the structural outcome.
